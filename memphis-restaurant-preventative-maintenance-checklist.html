Why Maintenance Matters in Memphis

In Memphis, our high humidity and hot summers put extreme stress on refrigeration and cooling systems. A lack of maintenance doesn't just mean dirty equipment; it means compressor failure, health code violations, and lost revenue during the dinner rush. Use this checklist to keep your kitchen running like a well-oiled machine.

Daily Maintenance (Front of House & Line)

Fryers: Filter oil and clean the vessel. Debris carbonizes and ruins oil flavor.
Griddles: Scrape and clean grease troughs. Empty and wash grease traps.
Ovens: Wipe down gaskets and door glass. Food debris prevents a proper seal.
Refrigeration: Check temperatures (Coolers < 40¬∞F, Freezers < 0¬∞F). Verify doors close tightly.
Dishwasher: Clear scrap screens and check chemical levels.

Weekly Maintenance (Deep Clean)

Ice Machines: Inspect air filters. In Memphis, dust builds up fast.
Walk-Ins: Check for ice buildup on evaporator coils. Ensure drain lines are clear.
Sinks: Delime faucets and sprayers to prevent hard water buildup.
Ovens: Removing racks and deep clean interior walls.

Monthly/Quarterly (Professional Checks)

Some of these tasks are best handled by a certified technician to avoid voiding warranties.

Refrigeration: Clean condenser coils. This is the #1 cause of failure in Memphis summers.
Ice Machines: Deep clean and sanitize the water system (remove scale/slime).
Gas Equipment: Calibrate thermostats and check pilot lights/igniters.
Door Gaskets: Inspect for tears. A $50 gasket can save $500 in electricity.
