Why Memphis Restaurants Fail Inspections

Cold-holding violations account for the majority of health department citations in Shelby County. The good news: most are caused by worn hardware, not mechanical failures.

Old, worn gasket vs. new correctly-sized replacement — the difference between a FAIL and a PASS.

Worn Door Gaskets

A torn or compressed gasket allows warm, humid Memphis air into the cabinet. The unit can't maintain 41°F when the seal is broken — and the inspector's thermometer will prove it.

  • Dart-style, screw-on, and magnetic profiles
  • Common sizes carried for same-day replacement
  • Proper sizing prevents premature re-tearing

Sagging Doors & Bad Hinges

A new gasket won't fix a door that hangs crooked. Worn hinges, bent pins, and failed self-closers are the hidden cause behind many "gasket" complaints.

  • Hinge pin and bushing replacement
  • Door closer adjustment and replacement
  • Strike plate alignment

Blocked Drains & Ice Buildup

When the drain line clogs, ice forms on the evaporator coil and airflow drops. The cabinet warms up, product temps rise, and the inspector writes the citation.

  • Drain line clearing and flush
  • Evaporator coil cleaning
  • Strip curtain inspection and replacement

Our Corrective Action Process

Step 1: Assessment & Documentation

  • Record current cabinet temperatures with calibrated thermometer
  • Identify the gasket profile type (dart, screw-on, or magnetic)
  • Inspect hinges, closer, and strike plate for wear
  • Check drain line and evaporator coil condition

Step 2: Hardware Repair & Replacement

  • Replace worn gaskets with correctly sized profiles
  • Adjust or replace hinges and door closers
  • Clear drain lines and clean coils if needed
  • Install or replace strip curtains on walk-ins

Step 3: Temperature Validation & Proof

  • Monitor post-repair temperatures to verify the unit holds 41°F or below
  • Document before-and-after readings for your records
  • Provide corrective action summary for re-inspection

Health Inspection FAQ

What causes most cold-holding violations?

Worn door gaskets, misaligned hinges, and blocked drain lines. These hardware issues allow warm air infiltration and prevent the unit from maintaining 41°F. They're maintenance problems, not mechanical failures.

How fast can you respond?

We offer same-day emergency service throughout Memphis, Germantown, Collierville, and Bartlett. We carry common gasket profiles on the truck for on-the-spot replacement. Call (901) 257-9417.

Do you provide documentation for re-inspection?

Yes. We document before-and-after temperatures and list every corrective action performed. You'll have a written record showing the unit is holding proper temperature for when the inspector returns.

Which units do you service?

Reach-in coolers, reach-in freezers, walk-in cooler doors and hardware, cold-rail prep tables, sandwich/pizza prep units, and ice machine bins.
