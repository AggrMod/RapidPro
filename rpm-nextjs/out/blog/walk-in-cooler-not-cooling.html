Walk-In Cooler Not Cooling? Here's What to Check

A failing walk-in cooler is every restaurant owner's nightmare. With thousands of dollars of inventory at stake, quick action is essential. Here are the most common causes and what you can do.

Before You Panic: Quick Checks

Before calling for emergency service, verify these basics:

  • Check the thermostat setting - It may have been accidentally adjusted
  • Verify power supply - Check the breaker and ensure the unit is receiving power
  • Look at the door seal - A gap can prevent proper cooling
  • Check for ice buildup - Excessive frost indicates a defrost problem

    • 7 Common Causes of Cooling Failure

    1. Dirty Condenser Coils

    The most common cause of cooling problems. When condenser coils are coated with dust and grease, they can't release heat effectively.

    Solution: Schedule monthly condenser cleaning or call us for a maintenance visit.

    2. Evaporator Fan Failure

    If you don't hear the evaporator fan running, cold air isn't circulating properly.

    Signs: Frost buildup on evaporator, warm spots in the cooler

    3. Refrigerant Leak

    Low refrigerant levels prevent proper cooling and can damage your compressor.

    Signs: Hissing sounds, ice on refrigerant lines, compressor running constantly Important: Only EPA-certified technicians can handle refrigerant repairs.

    4. Defrost System Problems

    If the defrost cycle isn't working, ice builds up and blocks airflow.

    Signs: Excessive frost on evaporator coils, ice blocking vents

    5. Failed Compressor

    The compressor is the heart of your refrigeration system. When it fails, cooling stops.

    Signs: Clicking sounds, compressor hot but not running, no cooling at all

    6. Thermostat Malfunction

    A faulty thermostat can cause the system to run too little or not at all.

    Signs: Temperature doesn't match setting, system won't turn on

    7. Door Seal Damage

    Even small gaps in the door gasket cause significant heat gain.

    Signs: Condensation around door, frost near gaps, unit running constantly

    Emergency Steps to Protect Your Inventory

    If your cooler fails, take these steps immediately:

  • Don't open the door - An unopened cooler maintains temperature longer
  • Check the temperature - Use a thermometer to monitor internal temp
  • Move critical items - Transfer highly perishable items to backup refrigeration
  • Document everything - Take photos for insurance purposes
  • Call for emergency repair - The sooner, the better
