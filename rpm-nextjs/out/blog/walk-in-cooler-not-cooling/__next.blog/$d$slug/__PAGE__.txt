3:Tf1c,<p><br/><h1>Walk-In Cooler Not Cooling? Here's What to Check</h1></p>
<p>A failing walk-in cooler is every restaurant owner's nightmare. With thousands of dollars of inventory at stake, quick action is essential. Here are the most common causes and what you can do.</p>
<h2>Before You Panic: Quick Checks</h2>
<p>Before calling for emergency service, verify these basics:</p>
<li><strong>Check the thermostat setting</strong> - It may have been accidentally adjusted</li>
<li><strong>Verify power supply</strong> - Check the breaker and ensure the unit is receiving power</li>
<li><strong>Look at the door seal</strong> - A gap can prevent proper cooling</li>
<li><strong>Check for ice buildup</strong> - Excessive frost indicates a defrost problem</li>
<h2>7 Common Causes of Cooling Failure</h2>
<h3>1. Dirty Condenser Coils</h3>
<p>The most common cause of cooling problems. When condenser coils are coated with dust and grease, they can't release heat effectively.</p>
<strong>Solution:</strong> Schedule monthly condenser cleaning or call us for a maintenance visit.
<h3>2. Evaporator Fan Failure</h3>
<p>If you don't hear the evaporator fan running, cold air isn't circulating properly.</p>
<strong>Signs:</strong> Frost buildup on evaporator, warm spots in the cooler
<h3>3. Refrigerant Leak</h3>
<p>Low refrigerant levels prevent proper cooling and can damage your compressor.</p>
<strong>Signs:</strong> Hissing sounds, ice on refrigerant lines, compressor running constantly
<strong>Important:</strong> Only EPA-certified technicians can handle refrigerant repairs.
<h3>4. Defrost System Problems</h3>
<p>If the defrost cycle isn't working, ice builds up and blocks airflow.</p>
<strong>Signs:</strong> Excessive frost on evaporator coils, ice blocking vents
<h3>5. Failed Compressor</h3>
<p>The compressor is the heart of your refrigeration system. When it fails, cooling stops.</p>
<strong>Signs:</strong> Clicking sounds, compressor hot but not running, no cooling at all
<h3>6. Thermostat Malfunction</h3>
<p>A faulty thermostat can cause the system to run too little or not at all.</p>
<strong>Signs:</strong> Temperature doesn't match setting, system won't turn on
<h3>7. Door Seal Damage</h3>
<p>Even small gaps in the door gasket cause significant heat gain.</p>
<strong>Signs:</strong> Condensation around door, frost near gaps, unit running constantly
<h2>Emergency Steps to Protect Your Inventory</h2>
<p>If your cooler fails, take these steps immediately:</p>
<li><strong>Don't open the door</strong> - An unopened cooler maintains temperature longer</li>
<li><strong>Check the temperature</strong> - Use a thermometer to monitor internal temp</li>
<li><strong>Move critical items</strong> - Transfer highly perishable items to backup refrigeration</li>
<li><strong>Document everything</strong> - Take photos for insurance purposes</li>
<li><strong>Call for emergency repair</strong> - The sooner, the better</li>
<h2>Temperature Safety Guidelines</h2>
<ul>Per FDA guidelines:
<li>Refrigerated foods must stay below 40°F</li>
<li>Foods above 40°F for more than 2 hours should be discarded</li>
<li>Document temperature readings for health department records</li></ul>
