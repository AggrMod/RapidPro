5 Essential Commercial Oven Maintenance Tips

Your commercial oven is the heart of your kitchen. Keeping it in top condition isn't just about preventing breakdownsâ€”it's about ensuring consistent food quality, energy efficiency, and food safety.

1. Clean Regularly and Thoroughly

The most important thing you can do for your oven is keep it clean. Built-up grease and food debris don't just affect tasteâ€”they can cause:

  • Uneven heating
  • Smoke and fire hazards
  • Increased energy consumption
  • Component damage
    • Pro Tip: Schedule a deep clean at least once a week, with daily wipe-downs of spills and splatters.

    2. Check Door Seals Monthly

    A damaged door seal can cause significant heat loss, leading to:

  • Longer cooking times
  • Higher energy bills
  • Inconsistent results

    • Run your hand around the door while the oven is hotâ€”if you feel heat escaping, it's time to replace the gasket.

    3. Calibrate Temperature Settings

    Commercial ovens can drift out of calibration over time. Use an oven thermometer to verify:

  • Set temperature matches actual temperature
  • Check at multiple settings (300Â°F, 400Â°F, 500Â°F)
  • Compare readings at different rack positions

    • If you're off by more than 25Â°F, call us for professional calibration.

    4. Inspect and Clean Burners

    For gas ovens, burner maintenance is critical:

  • Check flame color (should be blue, not yellow)
  • Clear any blockages in burner ports
  • Inspect gas connections for leaks
    • Warning: If you smell gas or see yellow flames, turn off the oven and call a professional immediately.

    5. Don't Ignore Strange Noises or Smells

    Your oven shouldn't make unusual sounds or emit strange odors. Common warning signs include:

  • Clicking or buzzing sounds
  • Burning smells (when oven is empty)
  • Excessive smoke from vents
  • Unusual vibrations

    • When to Call a Professional

    While regular maintenance can prevent many issues, some problems require expert attention:

  • Inconsistent temperatures despite calibration
  • Visible damage to heating elements
  • Electrical issues or sparking
  • Gas leaks or ignition problems
