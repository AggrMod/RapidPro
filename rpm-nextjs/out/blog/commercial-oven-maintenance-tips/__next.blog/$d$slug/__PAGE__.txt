3:Taf9,<p><br/><h1>5 Essential Commercial Oven Maintenance Tips</h1></p>
<p>Your commercial oven is the heart of your kitchen. Keeping it in top condition isn't just about preventing breakdowns—it's about ensuring consistent food quality, energy efficiency, and food safety.</p>
<h2>1. Clean Regularly and Thoroughly</h2>
<p>The most important thing you can do for your oven is keep it clean. Built-up grease and food debris don't just affect taste—they can cause:</p>
<li>Uneven heating</li>
<li>Smoke and fire hazards</li>
<li>Increased energy consumption</li>
<li>Component damage</li>
<strong>Pro Tip:</strong> Schedule a deep clean at least once a week, with daily wipe-downs of spills and splatters.
<h2>2. Check Door Seals Monthly</h2>
<p>A damaged door seal can cause significant heat loss, leading to:</p>
<li>Longer cooking times</li>
<li>Higher energy bills</li>
<li>Inconsistent results</li>
<p>Run your hand around the door while the oven is hot—if you feel heat escaping, it's time to replace the gasket.</p>
<h2>3. Calibrate Temperature Settings</h2>
<p>Commercial ovens can drift out of calibration over time. Use an oven thermometer to verify:</p>
<li>Set temperature matches actual temperature</li>
<li>Check at multiple settings (300°F, 400°F, 500°F)</li>
<li>Compare readings at different rack positions</li>
<p>If you're off by more than 25°F, call us for professional calibration.</p>
<h2>4. Inspect and Clean Burners</h2>
<p>For gas ovens, burner maintenance is critical:</p>
<li>Check flame color (should be blue, not yellow)</li>
<li>Clear any blockages in burner ports</li>
<li>Inspect gas connections for leaks</li>
<strong>Warning:</strong> If you smell gas or see yellow flames, turn off the oven and call a professional immediately.
<h2>5. Don't Ignore Strange Noises or Smells</h2>
<p>Your oven shouldn't make unusual sounds or emit strange odors. Common warning signs include:</p>
<li>Clicking or buzzing sounds</li>
<li>Burning smells (when oven is empty)</li>
<li>Excessive smoke from vents</li>
<li>Unusual vibrations</li>
<h2>When to Call a Professional</h2>
<p>While regular maintenance can prevent many issues, some problems require expert attention:</p>
<li>Inconsistent temperatures despite calibration</li>
<li>Visible damage to heating elements</li>
<li>Electrical issues or sparking</li>
<li>Gas leaks or ignition problems</li>
